About Me

My name is Jack Hamilton and I’m a Computer Science major and Data Analytics minor at Hendrix College planning on graduating in December of 2025. I’m passionate about quantitative research and turning data into decisions through programming and thoughtful analysis.

My journey has included many different projects and programming languages. In the past year, I’ve focused on learning industry-relevant tools while completing coursework in algorithms, scientific computing, and mobile software development. For the Spring of 2025, I did an exchange program where I studied Data Science and Computational Statistics at the Universitat Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona, Spain. I’ve contributed to independent research using census data to explore demographic trends, have done full stack mobile software development for clients, and I am currently an Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning Research intern at the Wake Forest School of Medicine's Center for Artificial Intelligence Research. My goal is to continue to learn different techniques, real world applications, and to get my Masters degree in Data Science and AI/ML.

Outside of the classroom, I’m a student-athlete, member of the Volunteer Action Committee, and on the Programming team at Hendrix. I enjoy applying computer science in practical and unexpected places. You can find more on my projects page or check out my GitHub, Kaggle, and Drive repositories.
